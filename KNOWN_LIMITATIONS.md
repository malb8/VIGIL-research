# Known Limitations

## VIGIL does not

* guarantee the origin, legitimacy or provenance of a navigation
* determine whether a destination is safe or malicious
* detect all phishing attacks
* prevent browser compromise
* prevent malicious browser extensions
* prevent malware operating on the endpoint
* prevent users from intentionally approving malicious destinations
* replace existing browser, endpoint, identity or phishing protections

## VIGIL provides

* workflow-context visibility
* navigation-context visibility
* destination visibility
* redirect visibility
* deliberate verification checkpoints
* targeted micro-friction at workflow trust-transition points
* opportunities for conscious reassessment before navigation continues
* domain familiarity signals based on local visit history
* identity flow and OAuth context signals
* homoglyph and lookalike domain detection
* composite risk findings that synthesize multiple signals
* redirect-chain visibility and trusted-redirector identification

## Important

VIGIL should be viewed as a complementary security control.

Its primary objective is not to block malicious destinations, but to make trust transitions visible and encourage deliberate verification before trust is transferred from one context to another.
